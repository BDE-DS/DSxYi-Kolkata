Curriculum

Four modules. Four capability upgrades.

No module is theory-only. Every block ends with participants building something live and walking out with it operational.

1

Imprinting Intelligence. Engineering Claude to Think, Write, and Decide Like You

Objective

Encode each participant's decision-making style, communication voice, and domain expertise into Claude so every output sounds authored, not generated.

Topics Covered
  • The credibility gap: why unbranded AI output fails in boardrooms, client decks, and partner communications, and how the Brain Imprint closes it
  • Structured voice capture: extracting thinking patterns, communication cadence, and domain instincts into a Claude-readable format
  • Velocity capture protocol: using advanced dictation tools to compress hours of voice profiling into a single focused session
  • Imprint architecture: deploying the Brain Imprint as a switchable Skill for clean voice separation across entities, clients, and stakeholders
  • Live session: participants initiate their own Brain Imprint build, leaving with a working Skill that makes Claude an extension of their professional identity
OutcomeParticipants leave with a functioning Brain Imprint Skill that makes every Claude output distinctly theirs, regardless of the business context it is applied to.
Claude SkillsClaude ProjectsVoice Dictation Tools
2

Precision Skill Engineering. Building Claude's Muscle Memory for High-Stakes Business Tasks

Objective

Build Skills that fire with surgical precision, execute without deviation, and scale across teams without requiring re-explanation or rework.

Topics Covered
  • TAR framework in production: applying Trigger, Action, Result logic to recurring tasks with real financial, operational, or reputational stakes
  • Trigger engineering: writing activation conditions and hard negative triggers that eliminate misfires, false fires, and scope bleed
  • Skill folder anatomy: the architecture of SKILL.md, reference.md, and executable scripts, and the precise logic for what belongs at each layer
  • Live adversarial testing: participants build a Skill, run it against deliberate edge cases, and iterate on failure in real time
  • Skill diagnostics: a structured framework for identifying why a deployed Skill underperforms and rewriting it to specification in under 10 minutes
OutcomeParticipants leave with at least one production-ready Skill and a repeatable engineering framework for building, stress-testing, and refining every Skill that follows.
Claude SkillsClaude Desktop
3

System Integration at Scale. Deploying MCP Connectors Across Your Business Stack

Objective

Connect Claude to the live operational infrastructure of your business and demonstrate real-time autonomous action across the tools your teams already use daily.

Topics Covered
  • MCP architecture unpacked: how Claude establishes read, write, and execution channels into external tools, eliminating the copy-paste workflow permanently
  • Live connector deployment: building active integrations across Gmail, Google Drive, Sheets, or CRM systems mapped to the room's actual operational stack
  • Permission architecture: designing granular read, write, and execute boundaries that keep autonomous workflows safe, auditable, and reversible
  • Real-time action demo: Claude pulls live business data from a connected system, analyses it, and executes a defined action without manual intervention
  • Multi-connector pipeline: chaining two or more MCP integrations into a single seamless operational workflow spanning the business stack end to end
OutcomeParticipants execute a live, multi-connector workflow that proves Claude can act across business systems at an operational level, not just respond inside a conversation.
MCP ConnectorsGoogle DriveGmailGoogle SheetsClaude Desktop
4

Autonomous Business Agents. Deploying Claude as an Operational Intelligence Layer

Objective

Move beyond connected tools into fully autonomous AI agents that monitor live business data, make rule-based decisions, and execute actions across your systems on a defined schedule, without human initiation.

Topics Covered
  • The agent distinction: what separates an autonomous agent from a connected tool, and why this shift redefines what AI can own inside a business
  • Agent logic design: structuring trigger conditions, decision trees, escalation paths, and output actions into a deployable agent blueprint
  • Scheduling infrastructure: building a time-based execution layer on Claude Desktop so agents activate, run, and deliver outputs on schedule without anyone in the loop
  • Risk and governance architecture: identifying which workflows should never run unsupervised, where human checkpoints belong, and how to build audit trails that keep agent behaviour accountable
  • Live deployment: each participant designs and deploys one autonomous agent workflow mapped to a real operational problem in their own business before leaving the room
OutcomeParticipants leave with a live, scheduled, autonomous Claude agent running against their own business infrastructure, executing decisions and delivering outputs without manual initiation.
